Why Low-Code/No-Code Builders Should Know How to Code

January 5, 2025

1. Customization

While low-code tools provide templates, manual coding allows you to add unique features that make your website stand out. For example, integrating animations or dynamic filters often requires custom JavaScript.

2. Debugging Skills

No-code tools are not error-proof. Understanding code helps troubleshoot issues faster. For instance, when a plugin doesn't behave as expected, you can examine the underlying HTML, CSS, or JavaScript to identify the problem.

3. Scalable Solutions

As your project grows, low-code platforms may hit their limits. Manual coding enables you to transition from prototypes to scalable applications, ensuring your website or app can handle increased traffic or complex functionalities.
